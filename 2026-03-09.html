Workout — Modified Heavy Snatch EMOM
Type: EMOM Time Cap: 20 min
Original Modified Why
1 snatch high pull (minutes 0-5) 1 snatch high pull (cap at 70% of 1RM snatch) Reduced load to prevent excessive Valsalva and intracranial pressure
1 hang squat snatch (minutes 6-10) 1 hang power snatch + overhead squat (cap at 65% of 1RM snatch) Split into two movements to reduce complexity while maintaining full range of motion
1 squat snatch (minutes 11-20) 1 power snatch + overhead squat (cap at 65% of 1RM snatch) Split movement reduces technical demand while preserving strength stimulus
Rx Notes: Load progression: Start conservatively and build by 5-10 lbs between phases only if movement quality remains excellent. Do not exceed 70% of 1RM snatch at any point.
Coaching Notes: Rest 15-30 seconds between the power snatch and overhead squat in later phases. If you feel any symptoms (dizziness, headache, vision changes), immediately scale load down or switch to muscle snatches.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • 2 min easy walk or slow bike (focus on breathing)
  • 30 sec shoulder rolls
  • 1 min overhead stretch (hands on wall, step back)
  • 1 min hip flexor stretch each side
  • 30 sec deep breathing (4 count in, 6 count out)
Intensity Notes: Load capped at 70% 1RM to prevent excessive intracranial pressure from heavy Valsalva. Movement complexity reduced by splitting catch position from pulling motion. Maintain RPE 7-8 maximum - this should feel challenging but controlled, never maximal. The single-barbell, single-load constraint is preserved while making the workout neurologically safer.

Workout — Modified Heavy KB Complex EMOM (Shoulder-Safe)
Type: EMOM Time Cap: 20 min
Original Modified Why
Minutes 0-5: 1 snatch high pull Minutes 0-5: 1 kettlebell high pull Preserves the high pull pattern without overhead component
Minutes 6-10: 1 hang squat snatch Minutes 6-10: 1 hang kettlebell clean + front squat Maintains hang position and squat component, eliminates overhead catch
Minutes 11-20: 1 squat snatch Minutes 11-20: 1 kettlebell clean + front squat Preserves explosive pull and squat pattern without overhead stress
Rx Notes: Use ONE kettlebell throughout. Start with moderate weight for high pulls, increase for hang clean+squat, then work up to your heavy single for the final 10 minutes. Focus on explosive hip extension and smooth transition under the bell.
Coaching Notes: Maintain the same progressive loading structure as the original. High pull teaches first/second pull mechanics, hang clean+squat adds the catch and squat, full clean+squat integrates everything. Rest the remaining time each minute.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Band pull-aparts (2 min easy pace)
  • Shoulder crossovers (30 sec each arm)
  • Wall angels (1 min)
  • Hip flexor stretch (30 sec each leg)
  • Thoracic spine rotation stretch (30 sec each side)
Intensity Notes: Replaced all snatch variations with kettlebell-based movements to eliminate overhead catching while preserving the explosive pull-under mechanics and progressive complexity. Single implement maintains the workout's structural integrity.
