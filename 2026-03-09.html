Workout — Modified Snatch EMOM
Type: EMOM Time Cap: 20 min
Original Modified Why
1 snatch high pull (Minutes 0-5) 1 snatch high pull at moderate load Keep same movement but cap load to avoid maximal Valsalva
1 hang squat snatch (Minutes 6-10) 1 hang power snatch Avoid overhead squat position which can increase intracranial pressure
1 squat snatch (Minutes 11-20) 1 power snatch + 1 overhead squat (separate movements) Break into components to reduce complexity and ICP spike risk
Rx Notes: Cap all loads at 75% of 1RM snatch. Focus on speed and technique over maximal load. Use same barbell throughout - load for your heaviest power snatch capability.
Coaching Notes: Rest 30-45 seconds between movements if needed. If you feel any head pressure or symptoms, reduce load immediately or switch to muscle snatches.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Walk or easy row (2 min)
  • Overhead reach and lat stretch (1 min)
  • Hip flexor stretch (1 min)
  • Box breathing: 4 count in, 4 hold, 4 out (1 min)
Intensity Notes: Key modifications: Removed squat component from snatches to minimize intracranial pressure spikes. Capped loads at 75% 1RM to avoid maximal Valsalva response while preserving the skill and strength development intent. Maintained single barbell constraint and progressive complexity structure of original workout.

Workout — Modified Heavy Single EMOM (Shoulder-Safe)
Type: EMOM Time Cap: 20 min
Original Modified Why
1 snatch high pull 1 kettlebell swing (heavy) Eliminates overhead position while preserving explosive hip extension pattern
1 hang squat snatch 1 single kettlebell clean + front squat Maintains complex movement with explosive pull and squat component, avoids overhead catch
1 squat snatch 1 single kettlebell clean + front squat + press (light) Progressive complexity building on previous movement, adds controlled overhead component with manageable load
Rx Notes: Use single kettlebell throughout. Minutes 0-5: Heavy KB swing (same KB for all movements). Minutes 6-10: Clean + front squat with same KB. Minutes 11-20: Add light press at top. Focus on building load during first 10 minutes, then maintain technique during final 10 minutes.
Coaching Notes: This preserves the single-implement, progressive complexity structure. The KB clean develops similar explosive pulling mechanics as the snatch variations, while the front squat maintains the strength component. The final press is light and controlled to protect the shoulders.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Cross-body shoulder stretch x 30 sec each arm
  • Overhead reach with wall support x 30 sec each arm
  • Doorway pec stretch x 30 sec
  • Hip flexor stretch x 30 sec each leg
  • Seated spinal twist x 30 sec each side
  • Deep breathing x 1 min
Intensity Notes: Replaced all snatch variations with kettlebell complex to eliminate overhead catching positions while maintaining explosive hip extension, complex movement patterns, and progressive skill development. Single implement preserves the workout's structural integrity.
