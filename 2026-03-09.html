Workout — Modified Snatch Complex EMOM
Type: EMOM Time Cap: 20 min
Original Modified Why
1 snatch high pull (Minutes 0-5) 1 snatch high pull Keep as written - good movement for building mechanics
1 hang squat snatch (Minutes 6-10) 1 hang power snatch Remove overhead squat portion to reduce intracranial pressure from deep squat under load
1 squat snatch (Minutes 11-20) 1 power snatch + 1 overhead squat Separate the catch and squat to reduce complexity and allow better control of loads
Rx Notes: Cap all loads at 75% of 1RM snatch or current comfortable working weight. Focus on speed and mechanics over maximum load. Build gradually across the 20 minutes.
Coaching Notes: Rest 30-45 seconds between lifts within each minute. If you feel any symptoms developing, reduce load by 10-15 pounds and continue. Prioritize quality movement over heavy weight.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • 2 min easy walk or slow bike
  • 30 sec shoulder rolls
  • 45 sec overhead reach and lat stretch each side
  • 1 min deep breathing in child's pose
  • 30 sec wrist and forearm stretches
Intensity Notes: Key modifications: Removed squat portion from hang snatch to reduce intracranial pressure, separated power snatch from overhead squat for better load control, capped intensity at 75% 1RM. Focus on movement quality and progressive loading while staying within RPE 7-8. Back off immediately if any concussion symptoms return.

Workout — Modified Snatch Complex EMOM (Shoulder-Safe)
Type: EMOM Time Cap: 20 min
Original Modified Why
1 snatch high pull 1 kettlebell high pull Eliminates overhead position while preserving explosive pulling pattern
1 hang squat snatch 1 hang kettlebell clean + front squat Maintains hang position and squat pattern without overhead stress
1 squat snatch 1 kettlebell clean + front squat Preserves explosive pull and squat pattern while avoiding overhead position
Rx Notes: Use one kettlebell throughout entire workout. Start with moderate weight (35-53 lbs) and build load each round. Minutes 0-5: KB high pull focus on explosive hip extension. Minutes 6-10: Add the catch and squat components. Minutes 11-20: Full movement from floor, emphasizing power development.
Coaching Notes: This preserves the single-implement, progressive complexity structure. The KB allows for the same explosive hip extension and power development as the original snatch complex. Build load conservatively - the single KB limits max load but maintains the strength-skill development focus.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Band shoulder stretches - cross body x 30 sec each arm
  • Band external rotation stretch x 30 sec each arm
  • Hip flexor stretch x 45 sec each leg
  • Thoracic spine rotation x 10 each side
  • Deep breathing x 1 min
Intensity Notes: Changed all snatch variations to kettlebell-based movements to eliminate overhead stress while preserving the explosive, progressive complexity of the original. Single implement maintained throughout. Power development and hip extension patterns preserved.
