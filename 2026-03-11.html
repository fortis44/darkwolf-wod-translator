Stimulus and Strategy: This bodyweight workout will challenge your core stamina in different ways. Push the pace on the run efforts, finishing each in 2 minutes or less. If needed, scale to either 300 or 200 meters to practice running at a faster pace. Scale the toes-to-bar volume first and then, if needed, substitute with knees-to-chests or hanging knee raises to maintain sets of 5 or more. If you can climb the wall fully without losing control or bending your elbows, but the volume is too high, reduce it to 5 or 7 reps. If you can't fully climb the wall without losing a straight arm, scale the range of motion. Workout — Modified Core Endurance Challenge
Type: For Time Time Cap: 25 min
Original Modified Why
400-meter run 400-meter row or bike (same effort) Reduces impact stress while maintaining cardiovascular demand
30 toes-to-bars 30 hanging knee raises (to chest level) Reduces intracranial pressure from full inversion while preserving core challenge
10 wall walks 10 pike walk-ups (hands walk up wall to shoulder height) Eliminates full inversion and excessive intracranial pressure while keeping shoulder/core work
Rx Notes: Scale hanging knee raises to 20-25 reps if grip fails early. Pike walk-ups should reach comfortable shoulder height, not forcing full wall climb.
Coaching Notes: Take 30-45 seconds of easy movement between each running/rowing interval and gymnastics set. If grip fails on hanging movements, break into smaller sets with brief rest.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Easy walk or gentle movement (2 min)
  • Shoulder wall stretch both arms (1 min)
  • Standing forward fold with bent knees (1 min)
  • Deep breathing - 4 count in, 6 count out (1 min)
Intensity Notes: Maintain RPE 7-8 on rowing/biking intervals (should feel challenging but sustainable). Scale hanging movements if grip fails or if you feel strain in neck/head. Pike walk-ups should feel like moderate shoulder work, not maximal effort. If any head pressure or symptoms arise, switch to seated core work like Russian twists.

Workout — Modified Bodyweight Chipper (Shoulder-Safe)
Type: For Time Time Cap: 20 min
Original Modified Why
30 toes-to-bars 30 strict hanging knee raises Eliminates kipping motion that stresses rotator cuffs
400-meter run 400-meter run No change needed - shoulder-safe
10 wall walks 10 wall walks No change needed - controlled movement pattern is shoulder-safe
Rx Notes: Strict hanging knee raises should reach chest level. Break into manageable sets (5-7 reps) to maintain quality. Wall walks should be performed with control - pause at top position.
Coaching Notes: Focus on controlled eccentric during hanging knee raises. Maintain hollow position throughout the movement. Wall walks should emphasize straight-arm strength and midline stability.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Cross-body shoulder stretch x 30 seconds each arm
  • Doorway chest stretch x 45 seconds
  • Child's pose with side reach x 30 seconds each side
  • Seated spinal twist x 30 seconds each side
  • Standing quad stretch x 30 seconds each leg
Intensity Notes: Only modified toes-to-bars to strict hanging knee raises to eliminate kipping stress on rotator cuffs. All other movements preserved as-is since running and wall walks are shoulder-safe when performed with proper control. Workout maintains same time domain and core/cardio stimulus.
MEDICAL DISCLAIMER: These modifications are not medical advice. The TBI version is designed for athletes 1+ years post-concussion who have been cleared for exercise. The shoulder-safe version is designed around bilateral rotator cuff tears. Stop immediately if you experience sharp headache, nausea, disorientation, or worsening symptoms. Consult your healthcare provider before beginning any new exercise program.
