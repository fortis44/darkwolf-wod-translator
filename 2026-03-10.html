Workout — Modified Dumbbell Intervals
Type: 4 rounds for reps Time Cap: 16 min total
Original Modified Why
40 seconds of dumbbell push presses 40 seconds of dumbbell push presses (same) Movement is appropriate, load will be capped
40 seconds of dumbbell deadlifts 40 seconds of dumbbell deadlifts (same) Movement is appropriate, load will be capped
40 seconds of double-unders 40 seconds of single-unders (1.5x target reps) Reduces impact and coordination demands while preserving cardio stimulus
Rx Notes: Use 15-25lb dumbbells (♀) or 25-35lb dumbbells (♂) - prioritize continuous movement over heavy load. Target: 8-12 push presses, 12-18 deadlifts, 30-40 single-unders per round.
Coaching Notes: Rest intervals remain 20 seconds. Focus on steady pace rather than maximum output. If you feel any head pressure or dizziness during overhead work, reduce load immediately.
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Deep breathing (2 minutes, 4-count in, 6-count out)
  • Standing forward fold with gentle sway (1 minute)
  • Seated spinal twist each direction (30 seconds each)
  • Shoulder and chest stretches (1 minute total)
Intensity Notes: Load capped to maintain RPE 7-8 throughout all intervals. Single-unders replace double-unders to reduce impact and coordination demands. The 20-second rest periods provide adequate recovery. Stop or reduce intensity if you experience any head pressure, dizziness, or cognitive symptoms during the workout.

Workout — Modified Dumbbell Intervals (Shoulder-Safe)
Type: 4 rounds for reps Time Cap: 12 min total
Original Modified Why
40 seconds of dumbbell push presses 40 seconds of dumbbell floor press Eliminates overhead position that stresses rotator cuffs
40 seconds of dumbbell deadlifts 40 seconds of dumbbell deadlifts No change needed - shoulder-safe movement
40 seconds of double-unders 40 seconds of double-unders No change needed - shoulder-safe movement
Rx Notes: Use same dumbbell weights as prescribed (35-50 lbs). Floor press targets: 8-12 reps per round. Deadlift targets remain 15+ reps per round. Double-under targets remain 20+ per round.
Coaching Notes: Floor press maintains the pressing stimulus without overhead stress. Keep elbows at 45-degree angle to torso. Lower dumbbells until upper arms touch floor, then press up explosively. Maintain same rest intervals (20 seconds between movements).
Cooldown (5 min)
  • Cross-body shoulder stretch (30 seconds each arm)
  • Doorway chest stretch (45 seconds)
  • Band external rotations (15 slow reps each arm)
  • Hamstring stretch (30 seconds each leg)
  • Hip flexor stretch (30 seconds each leg)
  • Deep breathing with arm circles (1 minute)
Intensity Notes: Only modified the push press to floor press to eliminate overhead position. Preserved the interval structure, dumbbell weights, rep targets, and metabolic stimulus. Floor press maintains explosive pressing power while protecting rotator cuffs.
MEDICAL DISCLAIMER: These modifications are not medical advice. The TBI version is designed for athletes 1+ years post-concussion who have been cleared for exercise. The shoulder-safe version is designed around bilateral rotator cuff tears. Stop immediately if you experience sharp headache, nausea, disorientation, or worsening symptoms. Consult your healthcare provider before beginning any new exercise program.
